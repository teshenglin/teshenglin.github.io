Sec.15.8 - Roller Derby

張創渝, 林胤辰, Te-Sheng Lin (林得勝)
Last updated on Jun 12, 2020 2 min read Calculus

Applied Project in Sec.15.8, Calculus by Stewart
Chinese version 滾動競賽

Suppose that a solid ball(a marble), a hollow ball (a squash ball), a solid cylinder (a steel bar), and a hollow cylinder (a lead pipe) roll down a slope. Which of these objects reaches the bottom first?

To answer this question, we consider a ball or cylinder with mass $m$, radius $r$, and moment of inertia $I$ (about the axis of rotation). If the vertical drop is $h$, then the potential energy at the top is $mgh$. Suppose the object reaches the bottom with velocity $v$ and angular velocity $\omega$, so $v=\omega r$. The kinetic energy at the bottom consists of two parts: $\frac{1}{2}mv^2$ from translation (moving down the slope) and $\frac{1}{2}I\omega ^2$ from rotation. If we assume that energy loss from rolling friction is negligible, the conservation of energy gives $$ mgh=\frac{1}{2}mv^2+\frac{1}{2}I\omega ^2 $$

Problem 1

Show that $$ v^2=\frac{2gh}{1+I^*},\quad \text{where } I^*=\frac{I}{mr^2} $$

Answer

By the statement, we know that $$ mgh=\frac{1}{2}mv^2+\frac{1}{2}I\omega ^2,\quad v=\omega r $$ By combining two equation above, we can get a new equation $$ mgh=\frac{1}{2}mv^2+\frac{I}{2r^2}v^2=(\frac{1}{2}m+\frac{I}{2r^2})v^2 $$ Therefore, $$ v^2 = \frac{2mgh}{m+\frac{I}{r^2}} = \frac{2gh}{1+I^*} $$

Problem 2

If $y(t)$ is the vertical distance traveled at time $t$, then the same reasoning as used in Problem 1 shows that $v^2=2gy/(1+I^*)$ at any time t. Use this result to show that $y$ satisfies the differential equation $$ \frac{dy}{dt}= \sqrt{\frac{2g}{1+I^*}}(\sin{\alpha})\sqrt{y} $$ where $\alpha$ is the angle of inclination of the plane.

Answer

Notice that $dy/dt$ is the y component of velocity $v$, so we have that $$ \frac{dy}{dt}=v_y=v\sin{\alpha} $$ Therefore $$ \frac{dy}{dt}=\sqrt{\frac{2g}{1+I^*}}\sin\alpha\sqrt{y} $$

Problem 3

By solving the equation in Problem 2, show that the total travel time is $$ T=\sqrt{\frac{2h(1+I^*)}{g\sin^2\alpha}} $$ This shows that the object with the smallest value of $I^*$ wins the race.

Answer

Notice that $$ \frac{dy}{dt}\frac{dt}{dy}=1\ (chain\ rule),\quad\frac{dt}{dy}=\frac{1}{\frac{dy}{dt}} $$ There for we can get $dt/dy$ $$ \frac{dt}{dy}=\sqrt{\frac{1+I^*}{2g(\sin^2\alpha)y}} $$ By solving this, we can calculate the total travel time. Notice that when $y=0$, $dt/dy$ don’t exist, so we need to approach it by using limit $$ \begin{aligned} T&=\lim_{x\to 0}\int^h_x\sqrt{\frac{1+I^*}{2g(\sin^2\alpha)y}}dy =\sqrt{\frac{2(1+I^*)}{g\sin^2\alpha}}\lim_{x\to 0}\int^h_x\frac{1}{2\sqrt y}dy\\
&=\sqrt{\frac{2(1+I^*)}{g\sin^2\alpha}}\lim_{x\to 0}\sqrt y|^h_x =\sqrt{\frac{2(1+I^*)}{g\sin^2\alpha}}\lim_{x\to 0}\sqrt h-\sqrt x\\
&=\sqrt{\frac{2h(1+I^*)}{g\sin^2\alpha}} \end{aligned} $$

Problem 4

Show that $I^*=1/2$ for a solid cylinder and $I^*=1$ for a hollow cylinder

Answer

For any object, it’s moment of inertia can be calculate as $$ \int\rho_m R^2dV $$ where $\rho_m$ is the density of mass, $R$ is the distance to the rotating axis.

For a solid cylinder, it’s volume is $\pi r^2 L$, where $L$ is length of the cylinder. Therefore the moment of inertia for solid cylinder can be calculate as $$ I=\int_0^L\int_0^r\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{m}{\pi r^2L}R^2\cdot Rd\theta dRdl =\frac{mr^2}{2} $$ Therefore, $$ I^*=\frac{1}{2} $$

For a hollow cylinder, we suppose that it’s internal radius $a\to r$ . It’s volume is $\pi L(r^2-a^2)$, thus the the moment of inertia of hollow cylinder can be calculate as $$ \begin{aligned} I=&\lim_{a\to r}\int_0^L\int_a^r\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{m}{\pi L(r^2-a^2)}R^2\cdot Rd\theta dRdl\\
=&\lim_{a\to r}\frac{m(r^4-a^4)}{2(r^2-a^2)}=mr^2 \end{aligned} $$ Therefore, $$ I^*=1 $$

Problem 5

Calculate $I^*$ for a partly hollow ball with inner radius $a$ and outer radius $r$. Express your answer in terms of $b=a/r$. What happened when as $a\to 0$ and as $a\to r$?

Answer

For a partly hollow ball, it’s volume is $(4/3)\pi (r^3-a^3)$, set $z$-axis is the rotate axis, so the distance to the rotate axis $R$ can be express as $\rho\sin\phi$. Therefore, it’s moment of inertia can be calculate as $$ \begin{aligned} I=&\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^\pi\int_a^{r}\frac{m}{\frac{4}{3}\pi (r^3-a^3)}\cdot(\rho\sin\phi) ^2\cdot \rho^2\sin\phi\ d\rho d\phi d\theta\\
=&\frac{m}{\frac{4}{3}\pi (r^3-a^3)}\int_0^{2\pi}d\theta\int_0^\pi\sin^3\phi\ d\phi\int_a^{r}\rho^4d\rho\\
=&\frac{2m(r^5-a^5)}{5(r^3-a^3)} = \frac{2m(r^4+r^3a+r^2a^2+ra^3+a^4)}{5(r^2+ra+a^2)} \end{aligned} $$ when $a\to 0$ $$ \begin{aligned} \lim_{a\to 0}\frac{2m(r^5-a^5)}{5(r^3-a^3)}&=\frac{2}{5}mr^2\\
I^*&=\frac{2}{5} \end{aligned} $$ when $a\to r$ $$ \begin{aligned} \lim_{a\to r}\frac{2m(r^5-a^5)}{5(r^3-a^3)}&=\frac2 3mr^2\\
I^*&=\frac{2}{3} \end{aligned} $$

Problem 6

Show that $I^*=2/5$ for a solid ball and $I^*=2/3$ for a hollow ball. Thus the objects finish in following order: solid ball, solid cylinder, hollow ball, hollow cylinder.

Answer

Solid ball is the situation $a \to 0$ in Problem 5, thus $I^*=2/5$

Hollow ball is the situation $a \to r$ in Problem 5, thus $I^*=2/3$

According to Problem 3, the object having smaller $I^*$ will arrive sooner.

Since $2/5 < 1/2 < 2/3 < 1$, the objects arrive in following order: solid ball, solid cylinder, hollow ball, hollow cylinder.
